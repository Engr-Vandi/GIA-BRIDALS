Bespoke vs Custom vs Ready-Made: What's the Difference?

Bridal fashion uses these words loosely, and it causes brides real disappointment. Here is what each term should mean, so you can ask the right questions in any store — including ours.

Ready-made (off-the-rack)

A finished gown, produced in standard sizes, that you buy as-is and have altered to fit. It's the fastest and simplest route. The trade-off: the design is fixed, the fabric is fixed, and your body is fitted to the dress rather than the dress to your body.

Custom

A custom gown starts from an existing design — one of our signature gowns, or a design you've fallen in love with — and personalises it to you: your measurements, your choice of fabric and beadwork, a different neckline, a longer train, a detachable overskirt. The skeleton exists; everything on it is yours.

Custom is the sweet spot for brides who've seen a gown they love but want it their way — and made for their body.

Bespoke

Bespoke starts from a blank page. We consult, sketch, source fabrics, and hand-craft a gown that exists nowhere else in the world — built entirely around your vision, your story, and your exact measurements. It is the most personal (and most involved) way to get a wedding gown, and the reason brides come to us from far beyond Port Harcourt.

How to choose

  • Tight timeline? Ready-made or rental.
  • Love an existing design but want it personalised and made to measure? Custom.
  • Have a vision no one has made yet? Bespoke.

Whichever route you take, insist on real measurements, a clear timeline, and every detail confirmed in writing — any serious bridal house will offer all three unprompted.
