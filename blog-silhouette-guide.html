Wedding Gown Silhouettes Explained: Mermaid, Ball Gown, A-Line & More

Before lace, before beading, before anything else — a gown is its silhouette. It is the single choice that most affects how you look, move, sit, dance, and photograph. Here is the honest guide.

Ball gown

Fitted bodice, full dramatic skirt. The classic fairy-tale shape, and the one that makes an entrance. Ball gowns suit almost everyone — the full skirt skims whatever you'd rather not highlight, while the bodice does the work. Consider the practicalities: a full tulle skirt with a long train is glorious in church and needs managing on a dance floor (a bustle or detachable train solves this).

Mermaid

Fitted through the bodice, waist, and hips, flaring below the knee. The most figure-celebrating silhouette there is — spectacular on hourglass and curvy figures, and the shape our hand-beaded lace mermaids are loved for. Be honest with yourself about comfort: a true mermaid limits stride, which is why we often pair ours with a detachable overskirt — ceremony drama, reception freedom, one gown.

Trumpet (fit-and-flare)

Mermaid's easier-going sister: the flare starts at mid-thigh rather than the knee. You keep most of the curve-hugging effect and regain your walk.

A-line

Fitted bodice flowing out gently from the waist in the shape of an "A". The great diplomat of bridal silhouettes — elegant, comfortable, flattering on virtually every figure, and the safest choice if you're deciding remotely.

Sheath / column

Falls straight from neckline to hem, following the body without exaggerating it. Understated, modern, beautiful on tall and slender frames, and lovely for intimate ceremonies and civil weddings.

The real advice

Rules are starting points, not verdicts. Brides regularly fall in love with the "wrong" silhouette and look breathtaking, because confidence photographs better than theory. Try silhouettes on — that is what a consultation is for. And remember that with bespoke, elements combine: a mermaid with a detachable ball-gown overskirt is two silhouettes in one day.
